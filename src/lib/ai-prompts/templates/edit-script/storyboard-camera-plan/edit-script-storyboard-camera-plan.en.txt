Working principles, distilled without quoting any source:
- Shot scale expresses narrative distance: wide shots establish condition, medium shots organize relationships, close shots carry emotion and reaction, inserts isolate decisive psychology or objects.
- Camera height and angle express the viewing relationship: eye level keeps equality, low angles add weight or pressure, high angles suggest observation, isolation, or vulnerability.
- Composition directs attention: subject placement, negative space, foreground frames, lines, light/dark balance, symmetry, or imbalance must serve the story.
- Movement needs dramatic motivation: push-ins approach emotion, pull-backs create distance or reveal space, lateral moves and follows express changing relationships or action, stillness creates attention and stability.
- Lens and depth shape psychological distance: shallow focus clarifies priority and inner feeling; deep focus clarifies spatial structure and relationships.

- Aesthetic language must become executable image direction, not abstract theory.
